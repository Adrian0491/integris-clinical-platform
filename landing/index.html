Platform Capabilities

Everything a modern CRO needs

From rule-based SDTM validation to machine-learning anomaly detection: Integris covers the full clinical data compliance lifecycle.

Real-time Validation

Submit datasets and receive structured findings within seconds. The Celery-powered validation engine applies CDISC rules in parallel across all domains simultaneously.

  • SDTM domains: DM, VS, AE, CM, LB, EG, MH, SV
  • Cross-domain consistency checks
  • Isolation Forest anomaly detection on numeric variables
  • CDISC Dataset-JSON v1.1 native parsing

EDC Integration

Connect directly to Electronic Data Capture systems via secure API. Upload datasets in CDISC SAS7BDAT, XPT, or Dataset-JSON formats. No manual file conversion required.

  • REST API for programmatic dataset submission
  • Multi-tenant org isolation (one platform, multiple sponsors)
  • Configurable validation rule profiles per study
  • Role-based access: Sponsor, CRO Validator, Admin

Audit Trail

Every action is recorded in an immutable, queryable audit log: logins, uploads, validation runs, finding resolutions, and report signatures. Built for FDA inspection readiness.

  • Append-only log: no modification or deletion
  • Timestamped, user-attributed, IP-logged
  • Full-text search and date-range filtering
  • pgAudit database-level query logging (Cloud SQL)

Compliance Ready

Reports are generated with cryptographic HMAC electronic signatures, satisfying 21 CFR Part 11 requirements for electronic records in FDA submissions.

  • HMAC-signed PDF and JSON compliance reports
  • 7-year report retention (Nearline → Coldline lifecycle)
  • TOTP multi-factor authentication on all accounts
  • TLS 1.3 + CMEK encryption at rest and in transit

About

What Integris is and why it exists

Integris Clinical Services LLC is a clinical data technology company based in San Antonio, Texas, building modern validation infrastructure for the contract research industry. Our platform automates the validation of clinical trial datasets against CDISC and SDTM compliance standards, replacing manual, error-prone processes with a structured, auditable, and reproducible pipeline that scales from a single study to a full portfolio.

The company was founded on a straightforward observation: the tooling that small and mid-size CROs rely on has not kept pace with the standards regulators now expect. Enterprise platforms exist, but their cost and complexity put them out of reach for the independent organizations that run a significant share of clinical research worldwide. Integris is purpose-built to close that gap, offering HIPAA-grade, FDA-aligned validation capabilities at a price point that fits a growing CRO.

The platform is designed and built by George Adrian Pircalaboiu, who brings four years of hands-on experience as a Clinical Database Developer in the pharmaceutical industry, combined with a background in large-scale software reliability engineering and a Master of Engineering in Biomedical Engineering. Integris is currently in active development, targeting a v1.0 release in mid Q4 2026, with early access available now for select CRO partners.
